KNOW YOUR STATUS

The only way to be 100% safe from getting exposed to any sexually transmitted diseases and infections is to perform abstinance. However, most people take part in sexual intercourse throughout their lifetime which is why it is important to know your status and to get tested regularly if you are having sex.


Get Tested

*Testing can be quick, eassy, and free.

*Most STDs can be treated with medication.

*However, some can't be cured only aleviated and contained.

How to know which STDs to get tested for?

If you've never been tested for STDs, don't feel awkward or ashamed asking about getting tested to your docotor. Most people get at least one STD throughout their lifetime and it is responsible of you to get tested in order to stop them from spreading further.

Impotant things to consider when consulting your doctor would be...

  1. Mentioning any symptoms you may have been experiencing
  2. Being aware if your partner has an STD or STI
  3. The type pf sex (vaginal, oral, anal)
  4. How sexually active you are, including the number of people

You test positive for an STD

If your results come back and you find out that you have an STD it's normal to feel embarrassed at first. However, you need to understand that most STDs are treatable and it is very important that you follow you follow your doctor's directions on how to properly treat it. It is very common for people to get an STD at some point in their lives which is why you should neither feel ashamed or alone. Many people live out their normal lives with an STD.
