ABOUT OILY SKIN

Oily skin is caused by excess skin oil produced within the pores. The primary test for determining if you have oily skin is when you start to feel some oil on your face. Most people can feel a little oil by late afternoon, but if you feel oil around midday, you have oily skin. Oily skin rarely reacts negatively to skin products like dry, sensitive skin types do. It has slightly better natural sun protection, but is also prone to acne.

PREVENTION
  1. Wash your face twice a day with a gentle face cleanser.
    1. This will remove the excess oil that's clogging your pores. Using a basic face cleanser every morning and night is the best way to prevent oily skin.
  2. Apply toner to tighten your pores and remove oil.
    1. There are many kinds of toner. Use either an astringent or freshener to fight oily skin. Stay away from skin tonics and skin bracers because they are only for normal or dry skin
  3. Use blotting paper and carry medicated pads for quick and easy oil reduction.
    1. Blotting paper is great because it doesn't dry out skin and it only takes 15 to 20 seconds. Medicated pads usually have salicylic acid or glycolic acid and are easy to use on the go. Since they're acid-based they're a great treatment for acne too.
  4. Use oil-free moisturizers and oil-free sunscreen.
    1. Read the ingredients carefully on any products you use. Choose only the ones that are water-based and noncomedogenic.
  5. Use a deep cleaning facial mask as needed to remove excess oil.
    1. Facial masks give you a deeper cleanse than an ordinary face wash. They go deeper to remove impurities and draw out the oil in your pores. However, there is a greater risk for over drying with facial masks. Don't use them often!
  6. Wear the Correct Kind of Makeup
    1. Read the ingredients list to make sure your cosmetics are oil-free and noncomedogenic. Try a mineral powder or water-based foundation, and powder blush and eye shadow rather than creams. And remember to wash off all of your makeup before going to bed.
  7. Keep Oil Away from Your Face
    1. Keep your hands and hair off of your face as both carry naturally occurring oils. Also make sure to clean your cellphone or telephone with an alcohol wipe daily.
