Mental Health

Mental health is important throughout your entire life. It includes emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects the way we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Some factors that contribute to mental health problems are: biological factors, life experience, and family history of mental health problems. There is a large stigma around talking about mental health. Though we cannot see mental illnesses, that does not mean that they do not exist. Nearly 60% of adults with a mental illness did not recieve mental health services in the previous year. That is a statistic that needs to change, and the first step is becoming educated. Fear of mental illnesses stems from the fear of not knowing. Nothing is more powerful than knowing, and becoming educated will enable you to no longer fear the unknown of mental illness.

Fast Facts About Mental Health

  • 1 in 5 adults has a mental condition. That adds up to over 40 million Americans.

  • 90% of those who die by suicide have an underlying mental illness. Suicide is the tenth leading cause of death in the US.

  • Suicide is the third leading cause of death in youth ages 10-24.

  • 50% of all lifetime cases of mental illness begin by age 14 and 75% by age 24.

    • What Can I Do?

    If You Are Suffering from a Mental Illness:

  • Seek help.

  • Recognize that it is not your fault.

  • Surround yourself with good relationships.

    • If You Are Supporting Someone with a Mental Illness:

  • Educate yourself about the illness.

  • Seek out resources.

  • Have realistic expectations, and understand recovery and treatment take time.

  • Septarate the person from the disorder. Love the person.

  • Understand that the illness of a family memeber is nothing to be ashamed of, and no one is to blame.

  • Provide emotional support.
